    • Do cybersecurity tools even matter?

    Short answer, they do! Think of your accounts like your house. Yes, thieves may find a way in but locking your door will significantly lessen your chances of getting robbed than if you left it wide open. You need to keep your cybersecurity habits up to date so hackers never find an opening in. Not knowing about cybersecurity tools and habits is the biggest vulnerability in your security.

    What to do if your account has ever been compromised

    If you suspect your account has been hacked, here are the steps you should take:

  • Update your security software, run a scan, and delete any malware.
  • Immediately change your password and the passwords of any linked accounts.
  • Look for tracks. Check to see what information the hacker may have found. You should take a look at the things you have sent on your email and the trash folder. On social media, check to see if any random accounts were followed and what messages may have been sent.
  • Inform your friends you have been hacked and ask them not to click on any links from you.
